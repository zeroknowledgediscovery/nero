Margaret had always prided herself on being rational. Her colleagues respected her methodical approach to complex legal matters, and clients paid premium rates for her analytical mind. The idea that she could develop an irrational phobia of something as mundane as ducks seemed absolutely preposterous.

Yet there she was, checking her phone for the time and immediately regretting it when she saw her screensaver – a photo from last summer's company picnic at Como Lake, where several mallards were visible in the background. Her hands trembled slightly as she quickly changed the image to something safer: her law degree hanging on the office wall.

The shower helped somewhat, the hot water washing away the remnants of the nightmare, but Margaret couldn't shake the lingering unease. She'd grown up around water, spent countless childhood weekends at Burnaby Lake with her parents, feeding breadcrumbs to the very creatures that now seemed to trigger some primitive fight-or-flight response. What had changed? When had her brain decided that waterfowl represented an existential threat worth losing sleep over?

Margaret's reflection in the bathroom mirror showed dark circles under her brown eyes, and her usually immaculate black hair looked disheveled despite her best efforts with expensive styling products. She applied concealer with the same attention to detail she brought to reviewing contracts, determined that no one at the firm would detect any weakness in her professional armor.

The drive to work took her along familiar routes through Port Coquitlam, past strip malls and coffee shops that had served as landmarks for years. She deliberately avoided the scenic route that wound past Lafarge Lake, though she couldn't quite articulate why. The radio droned with traffic reports and political commentary, providing a comforting backdrop of human concerns that had nothing to do with aquatic birds or their unsettling presence in recreational waterways.

Hendricks, Shaw & Associates occupied three floors of a gleaming office tower downtown, and Margaret's corner office on the fifteenth floor had been her sanctuary for the past six years. Sarah's response came quickly: "Everything okay? You've been weird about outdoor stuff lately. Are you feeling alright?"
