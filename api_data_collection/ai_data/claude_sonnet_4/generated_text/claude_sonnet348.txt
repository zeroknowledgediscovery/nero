They certainly didn't know about Zinnia Kupferdraht, who at precisely three inches tall, possessed skills that would make the most accomplished human detective envious.

Zinnia adjusted her miniature leather jacket—crafted from a single autumn leaf and treated with spider silk for durability—and peered through her magnifying glass at the crime scene. Three days earlier, the Pixie Council of Northern Germany had summoned her to their chambers deep beneath the Rathaus. The council chamber, hollowed out from the root system of an ancient oak that had somehow survived centuries of urban development, buzzed with anxiety. "Zinnia, we have a situation that requires your particular expertise," he had begun, his voice carrying the weight of authority earned through three centuries of service. "Someone has been stealing the Essenz."

The Essenz. Even thinking about it made Zinnia's wings shimmer with nervous energy. Every pixie community possessed a reserve of concentrated magical essence, accumulated over generations and stored in crystalline formations deep within their territories. The Hamburg pixies' Essenz had been growing for nearly eight hundred years, fed by the dreams of sailors, the hopes of merchants, and the countless small magics that occurred daily in one of Europe's greatest ports.

"How much?" Zinnia had asked, though she dreaded the answer.

"Nearly a third of our reserves," Elder Brombeere replied grimly. "And we know who's responsible."

The name that followed hit Zinnia like a miniature thunderbolt: Gestank Moderfuß. Her former partner. Her former friend. The pixie whose descent into dark magic had cost them both everything they'd once held dear.

Now, crouched beside warehouse seventeen with the evidence of Gestank's presence literally under her nose, Zinnia felt the familiar mixture of determination and dread that accompanied any case involving her nemesis.
