The morning light filtered through the greasy windows of Mel's Diner, casting long shadows across the worn linoleum floor where Lucie had been walking the same pattern for three years now. Back and forth between tables six and twelve, past the coffee station with its perpetually dripping pot, around the corner booth where old Mr. Henderson sat every Tuesday reading yesterday's paper. Her white sneakers squeaked against the floor in a rhythm that had become as familiar as breathing.

At twenty-two, Lucie felt ancient in some ways and impossibly young in others.
