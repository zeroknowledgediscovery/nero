Maya couldn't help but smile. Carla had been her cheerleader through every disappointment, every moment of doubt when the dream of making it as a performer seemed more impossible than ever. Growing up in East LA, the daughter of a single mother who cleaned office buildings to make ends meet, Maya had learned early that dreams were luxuries most people in her neighborhood couldn't afford. After they hung up, Maya spent the rest of the morning running through her routine one final time. "Miss Rodriguez? This is James Whitfield from the Britain's Got Talent production office. I hope I'm not catching you at a bad time."

"No, not at all," Maya replied, trying to keep the excitement out of her voice and sound professional.

This was her moment, her chance to prove that a girl from East LA could compete with the best performers in the world. Nothing was going to stand in her way.

She spent the afternoon making final preparations, confirming her travel arrangements, and trying on her performance outfit one last time. As evening approached, Maya found herself standing at her small kitchen window, looking out at the city that had been both her greatest challenge and her biggest inspiration.
