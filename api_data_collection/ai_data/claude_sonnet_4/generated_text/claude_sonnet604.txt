"Signora Marchetti?" Her assistant's voice crackled through the intercom. "Your ten o'clock appointment has arrived."

Giulia straightened her charcoal blazer and returned to her desk. "Send them in, Marco."

But these weren't real flowers—they were made from tissue paper, each petal carefully crafted and shaped by hand.

"Beautiful, no?" The woman looked up from her work, her weathered hands still moving with practiced efficiency as she twisted green wire into a stem.

Giulia nodded, surprised by how the simple creations seemed to radiate life and joy. "They're extraordinary. How do you make them so realistic?"
