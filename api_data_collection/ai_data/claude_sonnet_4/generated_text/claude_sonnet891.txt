Marcus had been rowing this stretch of river for three years now, ever since he'd woken up in Carle Foundation Hospital with a head injury that had stolen eighteen months of his life. He pulled harder, feeling the shell respond beneath him, cutting through the current with renewed speed. This was what he knew, what felt right in his bones even when everything else seemed foreign. The rhythm of rowing, the balance required, the way his body knew exactly how to move—these things had survived whatever trauma had stolen his memories.
