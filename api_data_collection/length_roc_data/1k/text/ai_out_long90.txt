They emerged into a space that defied description. No horizon, no ground, no sky—just distance given form. A chamber made of intention. Light coalesced into a pedestal, and on it sat an object no larger than a stone.

A key.

Not like the others. This one glowed not with stored memory, but possibility. Elias reached out. Mara touched his wrist.

“It’s not for you,” she said. “It’s for Anachem.”

The object responded, lifting into the air and dissolving into hundreds of threads of light that disappeared into the corridor behind them. And just like that, they were back.

The fissure hadn’t changed, but everything within it had.

The glyphs on the walls now pulsed in sequence. The stone beneath their feet felt warmer. And in the air, something new stirred—no longer a signal. A voice. Whispering.

“We see you.”
