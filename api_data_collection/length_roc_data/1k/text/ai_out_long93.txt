But Jude never showed.

Toby biked to his house.

Jude’s mom answered the door, looking more tired than usual. Her eyes were rimmed in red, and she held a crumpled tissue in one hand.

“He’s... resting,” she said. “He’s not been sleeping well. Nightmares, he says.”

“Can I see him?”

She hesitated, then nodded.

Toby climbed the stairs, already knowing what he’d find.

Jude sat on the floor of his room, back against the wall, knees pulled to his chest. His notebook lay open beside him, the pages filled with tight, strange symbols. Not words. Not runes. Just... marks, each drawn again and again until the pencil broke.

Toby sat down across from him.

“I dreamed of the mirror again,” Jude said.

“Did it show you the garden?”

“No,” Jude whispered. “It showed me us. Standing inside it. Watching. Not speaking. Just... watching.”
