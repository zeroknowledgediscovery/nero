That evening, a gentle rain fell—no thunder, no wind. Just soft, steady drops tapping the windows, soaking into the garden, washing dust from the trees. Tom sat in the living room, lights off, watching the sky dim. The notebook rested in his lap, a blank page waiting.

He picked up the pen and wrote:

Sometimes we don’t return from grief—we simply learn how to build homes in its shadow.

Then he closed the book.
