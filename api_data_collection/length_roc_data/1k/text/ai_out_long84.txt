They left the café together. The sun had broken through the gray sky, pooling golden warmth onto the sidewalk. They walked without a destination, their hands occasionally brushing, the silence between them no longer a gap, but a bridge.

When they reached the edge of the park, Marissa stopped and turned to him.

“So what now?”

Evan took a moment. He looked around—at the skyline, the trees beginning to turn, the city that had once held all his confusion and now waited to meet his new clarity.

“We make space,” he said. “For what comes next. For whatever it is that wants to grow.”

She smiled.

“I like that.”

And they kept walking, two people who had once collided by accident, now choosing each step together—not with certainty, but with intent.

Not a perfect ending.

But maybe, finally, a beginning.
