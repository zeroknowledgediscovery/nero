    The tree does not seek the world. It waits for the world to forget itself.

Elias stepped back. His heart beat too loud in his ears.

The lantern in the orchard flickered for the first time since it appeared.

He called a gathering.

Unusual in Rillhollow, where assemblies were reserved for trade disputes or festival planning. But the town came. They had learned to trust Elias again. Or perhaps they understood, on some level, what was at stake.

He stood before them outside the old well, Lyra and Cade at either side.

“There’s something growing beyond the ridge,” he began. “You won’t find it if you go looking. Not until you forget where you started from. But it’s there. A tree. And it’s made of everything we’ve failed to remember.”

Murmurs rose, confusion curling into unease.
