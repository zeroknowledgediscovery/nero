He didn’t remember arriving. Only that he had been walking. Fields. Trees. The occasional sound of water. The distant call of birds that seemed unfamiliar, though not frightening. His feet ached, but the ache was steady and manageable, as though his body had adapted to absence.

The town didn’t recognize him, and he didn’t recognize the town. A place of split roads and sagging roofs, porches sagging with the weight of years. Dovetail, someone had called it, though the name meant nothing. He passed a gas station where a man leaned against the doorway, eyes narrowed, a paper cup of coffee steaming in his hand. The man nodded as he passed, wary but not unkind.

The stranger nodded back. It felt like the right thing to do.
