The building was trying to speak again.

But not with words.

With form.

It started with the walls.

Not the obvious ones—the drywall, the paint, the brick behind it all—but the thin internal borders of the apartment that usually went unnoticed. The corners began to bend. Just barely. Enough that when he stood at a certain angle, they didn’t form a perfect right angle anymore. Instead, they curved inwards. As though the room was folding toward him.

At first, he thought it was his eyes.

But then he measured.

Took a carpenter’s square from the utility drawer and pressed it against the seam between floor and wall.

There was a gap.

Not large.

But real.

The next change came in the mirror.

The replacement mirror he had mounted in the hallway, once quiet, began showing not words, but movements.
