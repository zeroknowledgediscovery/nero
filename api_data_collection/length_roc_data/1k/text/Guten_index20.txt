 as I put thy mildness on, Image of thee in all things; and shall soon, Armd with thy might, rid heav’n of these rebell’d, To thir prepar’d ill Mansion driven down To chains of Darkness, and th’ undying Worm, That from thy just obedience could revolt, Whom to obey is happiness entire. Then shall thy Saints unmixt, and from th’ impure Farr separate, circling thy holy Mount Unfained _Halleluiahs_ to thee sing, Hymns of high praise, and I among them chief. So said, he o’re his Scepter bowing, rose From the right hand of Glorie where he sate, And the third sacred Morn began to shine Dawning through Heav’n: forth rush’d with whirlwind sound The Chariot of Paternal Deitie, Flashing thick flames, Wheele within Wheele undrawn, It self instinct with Spirit, but convoyd By four Cherubic shapes, four Faces each Had wondrous, as with Starrs thir bodies all And Wings were set with Eyes, with Eyes the Wheels Of Beril, and careering Fires between; Over thir heads a chrystal Firmament, Whereon a Saphi
