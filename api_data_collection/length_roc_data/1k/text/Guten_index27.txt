Your letter was excellently plain. I want you to hear nothing—not I.”  Bathsheba was unable to direct her will into any definite groove for freeing herself from this fearfully awkward position. She confusedly said, “Good-evening,” and was moving on. Boldwood walked up to her heavily and dully.  “Bathsheba—darling—is it final indeed?”  “Indeed it is.”  “Oh Bathsheba—have pity upon me!” Boldwood burst out. “God’s sake, yes—I am come to that low, lowest stage—to ask a woman for pity! Still, she is you—she is you.”  Bathsheba commanded herself well.
