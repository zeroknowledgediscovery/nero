Mina dragged an overturned bench across the opening, buying them a few more precious seconds.

They tumbled into the underground tunnels, the air damp and thick with mold.

For a long moment, they just sat there, panting, listening for signs of pursuit.

Nothing.

For now.

Eli slumped against the wall, the Rootbearer coin heavy around his neck.

Mina dropped beside him, wiping blood from a cut on her forehead.

Carrick paced back and forth, muttering to himself.

"What was that thing?" Eli finally croaked.

Carrick paused, rubbing a hand over his stubbled chin.

"A Debt Hunter," he said grimly. "Not a collector. Not even a Shatterborn. Worse."

He met Eli’s eyes.

"They’re drawn to Rootbearers," Carrick said. "Because you’re not just a debtor anymore."

He tapped Eli’s chest with two fingers, right over the coin.

"You’re a key."
