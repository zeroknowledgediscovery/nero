Chapter Six: Home as a Verb

By late August, the Texas heat had settled into its usual stubborn pattern—sunrises that scorched early and nights that clung warm to the skin like the memory of an argument. But Avery didn’t mind the heat anymore. She’d learned to move with it, to rise early before the sun got cruel, to work until the light grew syrup-thick in the sky, and to rest when the crickets took over the soundtrack of the land.
