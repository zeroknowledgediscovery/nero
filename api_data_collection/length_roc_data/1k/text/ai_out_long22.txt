Then he looked at Eli. “It’s your game. Finish it.”

The second half was like trench warfare. Every yard was earned. Eli ran more than usual, not by design but by necessity. The pocket kept collapsing, and he had to make plays with his legs. He scrambled for a third down early in the third quarter that left him limping. Cam returned, heavily taped, and pulled in two clutch catches to keep drives alive.

Midway through the fourth quarter, the score was tied 20–20.

Then came the fumble.

Eli dropped back on second-and-eight, pressure came off the edge, and before he could step up, the ball was swatted loose. Wisconsin recovered at midfield. The air left the stadium. Radley didn’t scream. He just put his hands on his head, then turned back to the defense.

The Wolverines held, miraculously. A huge sack pushed Wisconsin out of field goal range.

Eli got the ball back with just over four minutes to play. One last chance.
