Chapter Six: The Breach of Silence

The wind that reached Caer Lothewyn in the eighth year was not like the others. It came not with the scent of ash or rain, but with stillness. It passed over rooftops and stone towers without moving a shingle or curtain. It whispered beneath doorways and into dreams, and when it spoke, it brought no words—only the absence of them.

Corwin felt it first in his sleep. He had long since learned the rhythm of dreams, the way some echoed the past and others bent the shape of the future, but this was different. The night it came, he saw a great chasm splitting the sky—not fire, not storm, but silence. A rift not torn in the earth, but in the memory of sound itself.
