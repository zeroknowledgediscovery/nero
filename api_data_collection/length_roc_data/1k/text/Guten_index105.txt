She had been taken to Charmouth too, and she had bathed, and she had gone to church, and there were a great many more people to look at in the church at Lyme than at Uppercross; and all this, joined to the sense of being so very useful, had made really an agreeable fortnight.  Anne enquired after Captain Benwick. Mary’s face was clouded directly. Charles laughed.  “Oh! Captain Benwick is very well, I believe, but he is a very odd young man. I do not know what he would be at.
