June stepped out, towel around her neck. “We need to talk,” she said.

He nodded, already knowing the tone in her voice meant they were past the planning stage. This was the decision.

“We have a choice,” she said. “Either we keep running—north, west, into the Pacific if we have to—or we go back and end this the only way it’s ever going to end.”

“We don’t know what that means,” Cole replied. “We don’t even know if it can end.”

“But it’s moving now. Not just watching. Growing. That carving in the cave—it wasn’t a warning. It was a guide.”
