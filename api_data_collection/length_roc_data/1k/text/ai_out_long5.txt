 She knows what she was. And she doesn’t want to be alone again.”

Jacob stood, breathing hard, unsure if he should pull her away or kneel beside her.

“She’ll keep growing,” Ella said. “But she won’t take unless we forget her.”

Jacob swallowed. “Then we won’t forget.”

Ella nodded. “You have to help others remember, too.”

He looked at the tree. It shimmered slightly in the dark. Not threatening. Not welcoming. Just there. Alive.

“She’s not done with us,” Jacob said.

“She never will be,” Ella replied. “But she doesn’t need to hurt. Not if we leave room for her.”

For a long time, Jacob stood there with his daughter beside the tree, listening to the wind stir its branches.

Somewhere far away, something ancient sighed.

And the ground beneath them went still.
