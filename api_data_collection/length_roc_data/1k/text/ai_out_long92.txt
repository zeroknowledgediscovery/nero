Mara stared at the map. Dozens of sketches pinned with scavenged nails. Some featured angular spires. Others swirling glyphs. But every one of them held the same corner—three intersecting lines forming a triangle bent at one edge.

Always that.

Always there.

Spring returned like it had forgotten how to arrive.

The first green shoots came late and stunted. Rain fell out of rhythm. The soil along the east rim carried strange discolorations—fallout from decades of synthetic regulation—but the people coaxed life from it anyway. They used the old seeds and the vault soil and planted according to the oldest memory they had: instinct.
