One weekend, Elise invited him to an art fair. It was held in the parking lot of a renovated warehouse, with food trucks lined along the edges and rows of white tents covering booths filled with pottery, textiles, jewelry, and prints. The place buzzed with the low hum of conversation, dogs on leashes, the clink of iced drinks in plastic cups. Elise knew a few of the artists and chatted with them easily, her fingers grazing the rim of a ceramic bowl, admiring its glaze. Daniel watched her more than he looked at the art. She had a way of making people feel seen, even in passing.

They sat at a shaded bench afterward, eating spicy falafel and drinking mango tea from paper cups. Elise turned to him and asked, “Do you miss it?”
