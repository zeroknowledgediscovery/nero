Not new. Not registered.

She stood barefoot near the third tree, her clothes tattered, her hands pressed into the soil as if trying to hold it down.

Mara was the first to approach. The girl looked up as she came near, her face pale and too familiar.

Annie followed quickly and felt her breath catch.

She recognized the girl. Not as someone she’d met—but from dreams. From Lena’s old drawings. One of the names long forgotten, crossed out in old rosters, faded from attendance logs.

Her name had been June.

“Where did you come from?” Annie asked softly.

June blinked at her, slow. “From the between-place. The pit with no bottom. But it spat me back out.”

“Why?”

“She’s dead now,” June said. “Or changed. Or... consumed. There was nothing left to wear. No one left to forget. She lost herself and I was free.”

Annie knelt. “How long were you there?”
