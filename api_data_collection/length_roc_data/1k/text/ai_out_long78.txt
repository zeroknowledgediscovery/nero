Below the paragraph, a faint fingerprint in violet ink.

And below that: Talia, you are not alone. I am not gone. We are inheriting ourselves.

At the edge of the barrier, just before dawn, a new figure arrived.

He wore no coat despite the cold, his hair wet from snow, his eyes hollowed not by madness but by completion. He carried nothing but a strip of paper, fluttering in the wind like an unfinished message.

Talia watched him from a distance. Her breath did not fog. Her pulse no longer echoed with fear.

He stepped toward the barrier and paused.

Then he smiled.

And through the translucent shimmer, she saw herself—

An echo—

Already waiting on the other side.
