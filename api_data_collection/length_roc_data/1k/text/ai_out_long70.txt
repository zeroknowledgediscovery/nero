She stepped off into silence.

No one stopped her.

The platform beyond the void was narrow, slick with condensation. A single door stood at its far end. She opened it and found herself in a room filled with clocks—all ticking, none synced. The woman from the library was there, her newsprint coat rustling faintly as she turned.

“You’ve been writing,” she said.

Mara nodded.

The woman extended a hand, palm up.

Mara hesitated. Then reached into her coat pocket.

The pen was still there.

She placed it in the woman’s hand.

“You’ll get another,” the woman said. “But this one is done.”

The clocks began to chime. One after another. Then all at once.

Mara felt the world pull backward, like a breath drawn too deep.

And she was gone.

She woke in her apartment again.

No clocks.

No feather.

No mitten.

But the notebook on her nightstand had changed. The title page now read:

“A History of Forgotten Things”
