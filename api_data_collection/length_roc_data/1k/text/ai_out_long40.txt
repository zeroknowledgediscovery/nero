The pipes moaned differently in winter, slower, more introspective. Even the water itself seemed reluctant, emerging from taps with the hesitation of an old story waiting to be told.

Milo had always preferred autumn. That sharpness in the air, the way leaves clung to the last of their color. But the estate — and by extension, the system — was made for winter. Condensation stayed longer on the windows. The flow of memory through the pipes slowed, deepened. Emotions aged and thickened like sap.

He no longer needed to sleep in the traditional sense. Instead, he rested in pulses. Short, efficient silences. His body had become part of the filtration process.
