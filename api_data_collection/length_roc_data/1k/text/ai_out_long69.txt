 Librarian tilted its head.

A question, unspoken but clear: “Will you resume authorship?”

Eloise closed her eyes.

She remembered the pain of curation. The loneliness of being the one who decides what the world keeps and what it loses. She remembered choosing to forget herself rather than continue erasing others.

And yet...

She had seen what forgetting could do.

She had seen the cost of letting the wrong things fade.

Eloise—no, Elira—picked up the pen.

And she began to write.

Outside, the sky rippled.

The tear began to close.

Not by force, but by consent.

As the Librarian bowed, folding back into the light, the streets of Milltown stilled. The echoes withdrew. The hotel on Wren Street blinked once and was gone. The bakery door sealed shut. The firehouse engine aged before sunrise. The reflections returned to their owners.

Clara stood in the grass and watched the clouds mend.

She smiled.

Because the Archive hadn’t been defeated.

It had been given a new chapter.
