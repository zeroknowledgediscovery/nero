Kael approached slowly, the pages of his journal clutched tightly in one hand. He sat beside her, glancing up at the sails and the wind that now moved them without protest. “You were in the water for more than an hour. We thought we’d lost you. But then the shards flared. The entire sea lit up from beneath like someone had struck the ocean with lightning.”

“It wasn’t me,” she said quietly. “It was the Gate. It chose to close.”

Kael looked down at his notes, flipping to a half-finished sketch of the four shards fused together, ringed with runes that didn’t quite follow any known pattern. “And yet the Deep King rose. That part wasn’t in any prophecy. None of the lore ever spoke of him becoming real, not like that.”
