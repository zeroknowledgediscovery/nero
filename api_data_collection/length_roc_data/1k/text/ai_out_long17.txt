Chapter Six: Threads That Stay

Summer returned to Willowbend with its usual charm—a warm, syrupy slowness that made even time seem to pause and yawn. Lawnmowers hummed in the mornings, and the scent of cut grass mixed with charcoal and sunscreen. Children drew chalk galaxies on sidewalks. The corner store brought out its freezer display of popsicles. From the outside, nothing about the town looked unusual. And yet, beneath the golden rhythm of July afternoons, a quiet tension lingered like a splinter under the skin.
