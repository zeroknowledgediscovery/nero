Tom answered guilelessly: "It don't stand at all; it's all over. I had him up in court and beat him. Pudd'nhead Wilson defended him--first case he ever had, and lost it. The judge fined the miserable hound five dollars for the assault."  Howard and the Judge sprang to their feet with the opening sentence--why, neither knew; then they stood gazing vacantly at each other. Howard stood a moment, then sat mournfully down without saying anything. The Judge's wrath began to kindle, and he burst out--  "You cur! You scum! You vermin! Do you mean to tell me that blood of my race has suffered a blow and crawled to a court of law about it? Answer me!"  Tom's head drooped, and he answered with an eloquent silence.
