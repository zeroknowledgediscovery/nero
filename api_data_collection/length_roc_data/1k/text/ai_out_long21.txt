He passed the library. Its windows were lit now, the interior warm with amber glow. The door stood ajar.

It hadn’t been open in weeks.

He pushed it open slowly.

Inside, the shelves were lined and orderly, but not familiar. Not like before. The spines were wrong—books he didn’t recognize, titles that seemed carved more than printed. The Shape Beneath the Roots, When Smoke Remembers, Harvesting the Forgotten. The texts felt ancient, but they were bound in modern covers. The library had become a museum of memory, curated not by Ruthie, the long-retired librarian, but by the thing that lived behind the silence.

He wandered between the shelves until he came to the local history section.

There, a single book sat on a pedestal.

No dust.

No label.

He opened it.
