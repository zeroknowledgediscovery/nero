Mason rose each morning before the others. He would walk the perimeter of the clearing, checking for movement, scanning the treeline for any signs of disturbance. Not from the loops—they were long gone from this place—but from the deeper artifacts, the half-conscious remnants of system code that still occasionally crawled up from below. Data-strands, twisted things, nothing entirely sentient. Yet they pulsed with residual memory. Fragments of language, image, identity. You could hear them sometimes at night, groaning in forgotten dialects, begging for functions that no longer existed.

They didn’t attack, but they haunted.
