Some claimed it never stayed long on the branches of trees grown since the Root’s awakening, and that the footprints left in it vanished before the wind could take them.

Skrag found himself watching the snow more than walking in it. From the window of his burrow—expanded now to accommodate more scrolls than spoons—he observed the village in quiet, trying to decipher how much had changed since they’d returned from the Deep Grove. The surface of things remained the same. The blacksmith still bellowed over his forge. Children still shouted as they chased each other through alleyways. But something in the air carried a deeper calm, a readiness. The world hadn’t turned peaceful. But it had learned to listen.
