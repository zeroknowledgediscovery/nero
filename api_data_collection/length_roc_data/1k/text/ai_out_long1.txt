By dusk they reached the district—an innocuous patch of city, filled with aging apartment blocks, silent courtyards, and an abandoned postal station that had long since been converted into a warehouse. There was no cathedral. No church. Just the dull, humming air of an area that resisted narrative.

It took them hours to find the entrance. Beneath a rusted grate in the alley behind the warehouse, Marija discovered a staircase descending into dry stone, partially hidden behind crumbling brick. The air was dry and cold, too cold for a Vienna autumn. The steps descended further than they should have—deeper than city zoning laws allowed. Emil counted one hundred and thirteen before the tunnel leveled out, widening into a corridor supported by thick Romanesque columns, etched with symbols that flickered uneasily when caught in the edge of their headlamp beams.
