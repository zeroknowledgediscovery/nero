The storage unit was in a low-slung building behind a chain-link fence. The manager, a woman in her sixties with immaculate nails and a smoker’s laugh, led them down a hallway that smelled like concrete and history.

Unit 47.

The door creaked as it lifted. Inside, the air was stale and cool. Stacks of boxes, a few pieces of furniture covered in plastic, and a trunk with rusted hinges.

Elise approached slowly, her fingers brushing dust from a lampshade, a record player, an old steamer trunk. She opened a box labeled KITCHEN and pulled out a mug that read World’s Okayest Mom.

She laughed softly. “She used to drink peppermint tea from this every night.”

Jonathan stayed near the door, letting her wander. She moved through the space like a woman deciphering a map she hadn’t drawn, recognizing pieces but not the whole.
