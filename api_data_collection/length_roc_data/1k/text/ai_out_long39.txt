“A sealing script,” he said grimly. “Old Covenant design. They tried to contain whatever this place became.”

Marla's voice dropped. “And failed.”

The disc in Elric’s palm began to warm. Slowly, painfully.

Marla glanced at it. “You said it was deaf.”

“I thought it was. But maybe it was only waiting.”

A low vibration rumbled through the valley. Bones clattered against each other, echoing like a chorus of hollow chimes. The air tasted of metal. Then, from beneath the dust and bone, a voice rose—not spoken, not heard, but felt, the way one feels the approach of a thunderclap in the gut.

He fed us unknowing. We drink the name beneath the name.

Elric dropped the disc. It clattered on the stone, split, and bled black smoke. Marla backed away from the arch, her hand at her belt where her runes were etched in ink along lengths of cloth, ready to cast.
