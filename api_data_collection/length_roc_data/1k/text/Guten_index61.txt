From the moment when labour can no longer be converted into capital, money, or rent, into a social power capable of being monopolised, _i.e_., from the moment when individual property can no longer be transformed into bourgeois property, into capital, from that moment, you say individuality vanishes.  You must, therefore, confess that by “individual” you mean no other person than the bourgeois, than the middle-class owner of property. This person must, indeed, be swept out of the way, and made impossible.  Communism deprives no man of the power to appropriate the products of society; all that it does is to deprive him of the power to subjugate the labour of others by means of such appropriation.
