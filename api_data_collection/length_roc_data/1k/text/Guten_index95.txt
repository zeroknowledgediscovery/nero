But, if never--”  “Hush, hush!” and she pressed her lips to mine.  “If never,” I whispered, “you must take my place; you’ll be the only one of the House then. You must reign, and not weep for me.”  For a moment she drew herself up like a very queen.  “Yes, I will!” she said. “I will reign. I will do my part though all my life will be empty and my heart dead; yet I’ll do it!”  She paused, and sinking against me again, wailed softly.  “Come soon! come soon!”  Carried away, I cried loudly:  “As God lives, I--yes, I myself--will see you once more before I die!”  “What do you mean?” she exclaimed, with wondering eyes; but I had no answer for her, and she gazed at me with her wondering eyes.  I dared not ask her to forget, she would have found it an insult. I could not tell her then who and what I was.
