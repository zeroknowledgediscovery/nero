She titled the first one: If You Hear the Hum.

In it, she detailed everything.

Not just the events, but the sensations. The subtle signs that marked the Hollow’s presence. The emotional pull toward memory. The strange stillness in reflections. The pressure that built just before the patterns resumed. She wrote about the rules Isaac had taught her, and the ones she’d discovered herself:

    Mirrors are memories.

    Grief draws lines.

    Reflection is not truth.

    Interrupt the rhythm before it speaks your name.

She sealed the letter in a waterproof envelope and tucked it into the floor beneath her bed, beside the concrete slab. She marked it with a symbol of interruption—a jagged, inverted spiral. A broken circle.

Over the next week, she wrote five more letters, each addressed simply:

To the Next.

She didn’t know who they were for. But she had a sense they would be found.
