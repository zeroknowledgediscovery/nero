She turned off the screen.

That evening, she returned to the Charter Hall.

It was quiet now, its mechanisms humming in the background like the breath of a slumbering beast. She walked its length alone, fingers trailing against the bark.

For a brief moment, she considered leaving it behind. Power was always louder than wisdom, and the jungle rarely remembered its caretakers.

But as she turned to go, she saw three monkeys seated quietly at the far end: a young bonobo in a threadbare sash, a middle-aged squirrel monkey with ink-stained fingers, and a tired-looking mandrill with a child in tow.

“We heard you were still here,” the bonobo said. “We wanted to talk. About starting something. A council, or maybe a school. For those who want to understand the Charter, not just obey it.”

Kaia smiled, surprised by the ache that followed it.

“Then let’s talk,” she said.

And for the first time since the beginning, the jungle felt like it was listening.
