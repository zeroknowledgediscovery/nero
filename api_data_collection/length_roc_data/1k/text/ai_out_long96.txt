“Can we uncover it?” Mira asked.

“Not with hands,” the Ash-Crowned said. “With story.”

Elias stepped forward. “You mean retelling?”

“I mean remembering aloud,” she said. “The world reshapes itself around what is spoken in truth. Not just what is known, but what is held. You’ll need to return to the first fracture. The one that made forgetting possible.”

“And where is that?” Mira asked.

The Ash-Crowned looked at her, and something deep in Mira’s bones responded before words were spoken.

“The city beneath the sea,” she said quietly.

The Ash-Crowned nodded.

Elias closed his eyes. “Then it still stands.”

“It does,” she said. “Or it falls forever. In that place, time and memory twist around one another. The wound never closes. It was the first place to forget its name.”
