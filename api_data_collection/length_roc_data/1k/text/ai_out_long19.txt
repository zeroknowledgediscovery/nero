Inside the classroom, he took a seat by the window. The sky was pale and cloudless, and the light poured in across his desk in long, golden stripes. He opened his notebook and began to write—not a poem, not a journal, just a sentence to start the year:

I am here by choice, not by survival.

He underlined it, twice, and closed the notebook.

Claire was at a different school now. She had chosen to attend an arts-focused prep school twenty miles outside of South Ashford, her tuition covered in part by a grant and in part by an anonymous donation that her parents suspected had come from someone on the school board trying to make quiet amends. She hadn’t fought the idea of transferring. She wanted space—not to escape her past, but to own her future without constantly being measured against it.
