Victoria circled the desk like a predator, her pale eyes scanning Margaret's face. "You look unwell. Have you been to the state physicians?"

"Fatigue is weakness, Margaret. Weakness is..." Victoria paused, studying Margaret's complexion. "When did you last eat?"

The question caught Margaret off guard. She couldn't remember her last meal, though she'd been ordering food to her office out of habit. The thought of consuming anything solid made her stomach clench with revulsion. "This morning. Protein supplement."

"Your productivity has dropped fifteen percent in six months. Your staff reports odd behavior—missed meetings, incoherent memos, strange odors." Victoria leaned closer, and Margaret caught her subtle recoil. "There are whispers."

In the Dominion, whispers were death sentences. Margaret had signed those transfer orders herself, never imagining she might one day be their subject.

"I can explain—"

Margaret's grandmother had been a scientist before the Dominion banned women from theoretical research, claiming it made them too independent, too dangerous. Margaret clutched the letters tighter. "Because I've seen what fear does to people. The Dominion rules through terror, but terror is just another form of death. I'm already dead, Elena. I have nothing left to lose and everything to give."
