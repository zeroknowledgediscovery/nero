The morning dew still clung to the petals of the wild roses that bordered the cobblestone path leading through the heart of Glendale when Primrose Dewdrop first noticed something peculiar about her reflection in Mrs. Willowbottom's kitchen window. She had been hovering there for the better part of an hour, watching the elderly human prepare what appeared to be the most magnificent stack of buttermilk pancakes she had ever witnessed in her three hundred and seventeen years of existence as a breakfast fairy.

Primrose pressed her delicate nose against the cool glass, her gossamer wings fluttering with barely contained excitement as steam rose from the golden discs on the griddle. The scent of vanilla and cinnamon wafted through a crack in the windowsill, making her stomach growl in a most undignified manner for a fairy of her standing in the community. She was, after all, the official Morning Meal Inspector for the entire northeastern quadrant of Glendale, a position that required both sophistication and restraint.

But there was something wrong with her reflection today. Where she expected to see her usual cheerful countenance framed by honey-colored curls and adorned with her favorite dewdrop earrings, she instead glimpsed what appeared to be a much darker version of herself. The reflected Primrose had eyes that glinted with mischief rather than wonder, and her smile carried an edge that made the real Primrose shiver despite the warm morning sun on her back.

"How peculiar," she whispered to herself, backing away from the window with such haste that she nearly collided with a passing bumblebee who gave her a most reproachful look before continuing on his way to the lavender bushes that grew in wild abundance behind the Willowbottom cottage.

The reflection seemed to linger in the glass even as Primrose moved away, and for just a moment, she could have sworn she saw the other version of herself mouth words that chilled her to her very core: "Soon, dear Primrose. Very soon."

Shaking her head vigorously to dispel what she was certain must be nothing more than the effects of having skipped her own breakfast that morning in her eagerness to observe the human breakfast rituals, Primrose decided to pay a visit to her dear friend Buttercup Morningglory, who lived in a converted teapot beneath the old oak tree at the center of Glendale's fairy district.

The flight through the winding paths of the fairy quarter never failed to lift Primrose's spirits, and today was no exception. Tiny mushroom houses dotted the landscape like colorful polka dots, their chimneys puffing out sweet-smelling smoke that carried hints of cinnamon rolls and fresh coffee. Garden sprites tended to miniature vegetable patches while song birds provided a melodious soundtrack to the peaceful morning activities.

Primrose alighted gracefully on the curved handle of Buttercup's teapot home and knocked three times in the traditional fairy greeting pattern. The door, which was actually the teapot's spout painted a cheerful shade of buttercup yellow, swung open to reveal her friend's worried face.

"Oh, Primrose, thank goodness you're here," Buttercup exclaimed, her usually perfectly coiffed auburn hair appearing rather disheveled. "The most dreadful things have been happening all morning. First, the Pancake Grove's maple syrup has turned bitter overnight, then the Toast Valley's jam supplies have mysteriously vanished, and now I've received reports that someone has been deliberately burning batches of fresh croissants at the French Quarter bakery."

Primrose felt a strange chill run down her spine as she listened to her friend's distressing news. The fairy community of Glendale had always prided itself on maintaining the highest standards of breakfast excellence, both for their own enjoyment and for their sacred duty of inspiring human breakfast makers throughout the region. An attack on breakfast was an attack on everything they held dear.

"This sounds like the work of someone who truly understands our ways," Primrose mused, accepting the cup of chamomile tea that Buttercup offered her. "Only another breakfast fairy would know exactly how to cause maximum disruption to our morning meal systems."

Buttercup nodded gravely, settling into her favorite armchair which had been fashioned from a large walnut shell and cushioned with dandelion fluff. "That's precisely what worries me most, dear friend. The Council of Morning Meals has called an emergency session for this afternoon, and they're asking you to lead the investigation."

The honor of being selected for such an important task should have filled Primrose with pride, but instead she found herself experiencing an odd sensation of unease that she couldn't quite explain. Images flashed through her mind of darkened bakeries and spoiled breakfast ingredients, but they felt less like imagination and more like memories that didn't quite belong to her.

"Of course I'll help," she heard herself saying, though her voice sounded strangely distant to her own ears. "Whoever is behind these attacks on breakfast will not succeed while I draw breath."

As she spoke these words, Primrose caught sight of her reflection in Buttercup's polished silver tea service, and once again she saw that darker version of herself looking back.
