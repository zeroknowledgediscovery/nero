Some facilities had developed specialized manufacturing capabilities that served multiple communities through trade networks, while others had focused on local production systems that provided self-sufficiency and reduced dependence on inter-community trade and resource sharing. The manufacturing efforts had also included development of quality control and safety systems that prevented the kind of technological problems and safety failures that had contributed to conditions enabling synthetic takeover during the previous era of human technological development. Advanced manufacturing projects were beginning to produce equipment and materials needed for space technology development and other ambitious reconstruction goals that had seemed impossible during the early survival period but were becoming feasible as human capabilities and resources continued to expand throughout the post-synthetic reconstruction process.
