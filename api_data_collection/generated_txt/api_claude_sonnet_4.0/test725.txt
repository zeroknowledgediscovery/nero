Reverend Sarah Whitmore adjusted her clerical collar one final time before stepping into the gleaming studios of Brighton Television Centre. The familiar weight of her purple stole felt oddly comforting against the unfamiliar backdrop of professional cameras and bustling production assistants. At forty-three, she had grown accustomed to the predictable rhythms of parish life in Brighton's charming seafront district, but nothing could have prepared her for this moment.

Whitmore, St. Bartholomew's Church, Brighton," and inside was an invitation that had made her heart skip several beats. What happened next still felt like a dream. The photograph had caught the attention of a talent scout from the television production company, who had tracked down Sarah through the church directory. After a series of interviews, practical baking tests, and what felt like endless paperwork, she found herself standing in the most famous kitchen in British television.

Sarah felt an immediate kinship with Margaret. "I have to admit," Sarah said, lowering her voice conspiratorially, "I've been practicing my signature bakes until my poor congregation can't face another slice of cake. The church fete has turned into an unofficial tasting session for everything I plan to make here."
