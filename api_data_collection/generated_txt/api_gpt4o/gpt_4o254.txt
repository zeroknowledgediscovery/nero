Nestled in the heartland of America, in a world unseen by human eyes, Kansas City harbored more than the typical hustling and bustling of urban life. Oh, the terror they evoked in his small heart! Despite their unassuming wobbly walk and constant quacking, Linbog was sure they were manifestations of some ancient evil intent on his destruction. His fellow denizens found endless delight in quacking noises that would have sent Linbog scurrying to his subterranean hole, ears wrapped in whatever mossy insulation he could find.

Grotmol, a bulbous goblin with an unrivaled scent and an unkindness matched only by his determination to thwart Linbog's rare bursts of bravery.

"Linbog!" bellowed Grotmol, storming into Linbog's meticulously organized grotto. "By the dusty corners of the under-roots, are you still fretting over those silly ducks?"

"I could venture there," Linbog returned, surprising himself with a flicker of resolve. "If not for..." He hesitated, casting a wary glance downward.

"Merely?" The vendor chuckled, a dry sound akin to leaves rustling in a dying autumn. "Not so; it has history, perhaps a secret—much like yourself. Beware the layers of what you hold, young one. Within lies a truth dressed as fiction."

The vendor's smile was curiously enigmatic. "Courage, perhaps? Sometimes the smallest things wield the greatest strength in the story. Remember this, Linbog; it hides yet shares."
