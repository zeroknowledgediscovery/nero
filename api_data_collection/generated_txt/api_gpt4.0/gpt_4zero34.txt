Over the ages, operating systems have transitioned from obscure code hidden behind the scenes to being the bridge between man and machine. Each update, every new version, seems like an attempt to demystify the human intelligence behind its creation, yet, for the layman, it only becomes more enigmatic.

The early designs of operating systems were simple; their main task was to manage hardware resources and provide an environment in which a user could execute programs. The mystery was not layered as it is today. The early OS, like the UNIX and DOS, had contemplative silence around it, primarily because they were used by a niche community of tech enthusiasts.

Over time, as computers became household necessities, the core of the operating system began to throb with an intensity, densely encapsulated by various other modules and features. The Windows OS introduced GUI (Graphical User Interface), a pivotal point in the history of operating systems. It was as if the OS had suddenly learned to communicate with its users in a language they understood. Yet, despite being more user-friendly, it was an enigma in its right. How did clicking an icon, dragged to a box marked ‘Trash’, delete files? 

As the era of mobile computing dawned, operating systems began to be designed for smaller, more portable devices. The introduction of Android and iOS heralded the age of wireless computing, and we found ourselves swimming with the tide. Here was an OS that lived inside our pockets, one that we depended on for communication, navigation, entertainment, and even fitness tracking. The OS had morphed into a more personal device, yet remained an enigma. 

The mystery deepened with time. Consider the evolution of the internet of things (IoT) and the role of embedded operating systems. We now have OS in our smart homes controlling our lighting, home security, and even our refrigerators. The OS had infiltrated our lives in ways we could never have imagined, making it both an essential companion and a perpetual enigma. 

Even as we celebrate the convenience brought to us by these advancements, the OS remains one of the biggest enigmas of all. Its evolution has been simultaneously fascinating and perplexing. The OS of today is not just code. It's a manifestation of collective human intellect, striving for a seamless partnership between man and machine. 

But despite this push towards accessibility and user-friendliness, the OS remains shrouded in its mystical aura. For the everyday user, the inner workings of an OS remain as distant and unknowable as the furthest stars. We use it, depend on it, and paradoxically, remain mystified by it. The metamorphosis of constitutional evolution is a testament to the adaptive and innovative nature of societal structures. Its development is a continuous process that keeps pace with the evolving ideals and aspirations of the people, law, and society.

The inception of a constitution marks the genesis of its evolutionary journey. Originally, these hallowed papers were bare, only containing fundamental principles to guide society. As societies expanded, so too did the complexity and reach of their constitutions. The document graduated from a mere guideline to an inalienable document encapsulating a nation's beliefs, mores, and operational mechanism.

However, the evolutionary vigor of a constitution is not solely due to the changing nature of societal structures. Its metamorphosis is also a product of the efforts to rectify faults or imperfections in previous provisions, reshape laws to address unforeseen circumstances or challenges, or recalibrate the balance of power.

One of the hallmarks of this transformative process is the introduction of amendments. Modifications to the constitution serve not only to remedy misalignments with the existing social fabric, but also pave the way for society's future. While on the surface, amendments might appear as standalone evolutions, each change is a brick in the continually expanding structure of constitutional architecture. 

Unanticipated occurrences, whether social, political, or even technological, play a significant role in catalyzing constitutional evolution. In the face of such changes, failure to adapt might result in societal stagnation, political upheaval, or a massive shift in societal values. An effective constitution, therefore, has the uncanny ability to reflect the zeitgeist of the time and adapt swiftly to these changes. 

Yet, it's important to note that not all constitutional changes are benevolent. Amendments might not always be in the best interest of all members of society. Often, power dynamics play an integral role in determining the direction of constitutional evolution. It underscores the importance of vigilance and active participation from all societal parties to ensure that the constitution evolves in a manner that is just, equitable, and representative of the collective will.

The development and growth of constitutional evolution are never linear or absolute. Changes don't always reflect societal progress, and regressions are not uncommon. At times, the evolution might seem slow, marked by periods of stagnation, while at others, rapid advancements might transpire. Nonetheless, the evolutionary process persists, with the constitution continuously being molded and remodeled by the intricate interplay of various forces and dynamics.

Ultimately, constitutional evolution is a manifestation of a nation's journey. It's a chronicle of a society's struggles, victories, and aspirations. It serves as a mirror reflecting not only what the society is, but also what it aspires to become. As the wheels of time creak ever forward, humanity finds itself standing on the precipice of a world defined by artificial intelligence (AI). However, society's understanding of its place in this unfolding narrative remains hazy, the future obscured by the breathless pace of technological evolution.

While AI's fingerprints have touched almost every sphere of human life, the framework of society remains stubbornly human-centric. Privilege, power, hierarchy, societal norms, and national boundaries delineate our lived experiences. Nevertheless, the relentless march of AI technology, permeating our everyday lives, is challenging this status quo, subtly reshaping the societal structure and stirring the winds of change.

In our quest for an AI-society, the first barricade we face is that of acceptance. The fear of automation taking away jobs, the unease of being submerged under a wave of unhuman intelligence – this technological anxiety many of us feel is real. Yet it is here where we must first understand that AI is not an enemy pitted against human progress. Rather, it is the malleable clay in our hands – a tool we shape according to our needs, desires, and values.

Just as the first fire must have terrified our early ancestors yet eventually became the cornerstone of human civilization, AI may terrify us now with its untamed power and potential, but it also holds the promise of a society where mundane tasks are automated, freeing humans to explore more complex and critical thinking responsibilities.

At its core, one could argue that AI is about democratization – providing access to intelligent services to everyone, irrespective of who they are or where they come from. Think of the rural farmer who can now optimize his crop yield through data-driven AI, or the high school student who can access personalized tutoring at her fingertips. In this sense, AI is less a threat to societal norms and more an equalizer, levelling the playing field for all.

However, the prerogative of ensuring that AI serves a democratizing role falls upon those at the helm of its creation and application. Central to this is the recognition that the usage of AI should not amount to replacing human intelligence but rather augmenting it. Therefore, our foray into an AI-centric society necessitates a new paradigm of thinking – one that sees human and AI as collaborators rather than adversaries.

This cultural shift is as necessary as it is challenging. Institutions will need to scrutinize antiquated systems that promote inequality and exclusion while nurturing an environment where technology serves the masses rather than a select few. Education, often seen as the passport to the future, will need an overhaul for a generation preparing to coexist with AI.

Simultaneously, as AI inches closer to our personal spheres – narrating bedtime stories to our children, keeping a watchful eye on our elderly – ethical considerations crop up. Who bears responsibility when an AI missteps? How much autonomy are we willing to surrender to these intelligent systems? How do we ensure that the AI mirrors the diversity of the society it serves and not the biases of its creators? After our extensive exploration of the cosmos' creation, it is now time to delve into its development. The evolution of the cosmos is a topic of intense debate and study among scientists and researchers worldwide. It is important to note that our understanding of the cosmos’ evolution is always evolving, adapting to new evidence and observations.

As we dive into this topic, we are caught by the continual cosmic expansion, which commenced in the flash of the Big Bang. The Big Bang theory remains the most widely accepted explanation for the origins and evolution of the cosmos. It suggests that - nearly 14 billion years ago - the universe originated from a singular point of infinite density and temperature, before embarking on its inflationary course.

The cosmos' evolution is more than just raw matter and energy. The cosmos is the stage where the drama of life unfolds, and life - as far as we know - is a significant part of the cosmos' evolution. However, while we understand how planets, stars, and galaxies interact, the exact origin of life remains the subject of disagreement. Theories range from life emerging spontaneously on Earth through abiogenesis, to hypotheses about panspermia, where life spreads between planets and even star systems.

Another interesting chapter in the cosmos' evolution story involves galaxies. Galaxies are formations of billions of stars, along with considerable amounts of gas and dust. The universe's violent infancy, filled with high-energy radiation, and the current relatively calm age, represents a tale of survival and restructuring in the face of change. As these congregations of stars, galaxies, the products of the cosmos' evolution, slowly evolve and shape the universe we know today.

Furthermore, the evolution of the cosmos can be seen in the cosmic microwave background radiation (CMB). CMB is an eminently important relic from a time when the universe was merely a few hundred thousand years old. It represents the flash of the Big Bang, frozen in time and spread across the universe. Detailed studies of the cosmic radiation have confirmed our Big Bang theories and refined our understanding of cosmic expansion.

Yet, the evolution of the cosmos doesn't stop there. It encompasses dark matter and dark energy, which constitute around 95% of the universe yet remain fundamentally unexplained. These are among the grandest mysteries of cosmic evolution, representing spaces where we grasp for understanding. The distribution of dark matter and the influence of dark energy on cosmic expansion rates currently remain as the edge of the cosmos' evolutionary tale.

Ultimate questions remain about the cosmos' future evolution. Will the universe keep on expanding indefinitely or will it eventually collapse back onto itself? The answer to this lies in the delicate balance between dark energy propelling the cosmos apart, and gravity trying to pull it all back together.
