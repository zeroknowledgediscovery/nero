Nestled amongst a stand of tall pines, it looked out over the immense, frozen surface of Lake Ontario, which stretched to the horizon like a sheet of hammered pewter. There was a quiet humility to the design, a sense of peace and permanence that was absent from all the brilliant, fortified structures he had built in Jakarta. This was not the work of an architect for criminals; this was the work of a man who was building a place to hide his soul.

It was not a grief for the lie she had lived, but a new, sharper grief for the truth she had just been given—the truth of a future that had been stolen from them both. She looked out at the vast, frozen lake, at the clean, quiet world Banyu had built for her. He had given her an escape, a weapon, and now, a home. He had given her a choice. Not just about vengeance, but about who she would be. Anna Rostova, the ghost living in a sterile downtown apartment, or Elara, the woman standing in a house built of secrets and love, on a road called Solitude Path.
