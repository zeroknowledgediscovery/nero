The coal mines that had once provided the Ashworth fortune had been sold off piece by piece, and now even the servants had begun to whisper about seeking employment elsewhere.

"Another letter from Hartwell and Sons," muttered Edmund, breaking the wax seal with fingers that trembled slightly. The contents were predictably grim—a final notice before legal proceedings would commence. He set it aside with the others, each one a nail in the coffin of his family's reputation.

The wind howled through the chimney, causing the fire to sputter and dance erratically. Edmund pulled his worn dressing gown tighter around his shoulders and contemplated the irony of his situation.
