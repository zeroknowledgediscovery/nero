"Congratulations Sarah! You have been selected as one of the final ten contestants for X-Factor Season 15. Please report to the Sony Music Studios in Los Angeles on Monday, September 12th at 9 AM sharp for final preparations."

Ten contestants. Out of hundreds of thousands who had auditioned across the country, she was one of ten. Sarah sank into the secondhand armchair she'd bought from a garage sale last month, the springs creaking under her weight. At twenty-four, she'd already felt like her dreams were slipping away, especially after little Emma had arrived eight months ago. But here it was – her shot, her one real chance to make something of herself.

Emma stirred in her crib, making those soft cooing sounds that always melted Sarah's heart. The baby had her father's dark eyes, though Marcus had never stuck around long enough to see them open for the first time. Sarah had been three months pregnant when he'd decided that fatherhood wasn't part of his "artistic vision" and disappeared to Nashville with some country singer who'd promised him a record deal.

"Mama's gonna be on TV, baby girl," Sarah whispered, lifting Emma from the crib and settling back into the chair. "Maybe we can finally get out of this place. Get you a real nursery, with those glow-in-the-dark stars on the ceiling like I always wanted when I was little."

The apartment was barely four hundred square feet, and the walls were thin enough that she could hear Mrs. Chen next door practicing her morning tai chi routine. But it was theirs, and Sarah had worked three jobs to afford even this modest space. Waitressing at Denny's during the day, cleaning office buildings at night, and singing at the Rusty Anchor on weekends – a dive bar where the clientele was more interested in their beer than her rendition of Adele covers.

Sarah's stomach lurched. Media attention was part of the package, she knew that, but she'd hoped to have a few days to process everything before the world found out. She fumbled for the remote and switched on the small television perched precariously on a stack of milk crates.

"...and in entertainment news, Overland Park native Sarah Martinez has secured her spot in the final ten contestants of this season's X-Factor competition. Victoria Sterling. The name hit Sarah like a physical blow, and Emma sensed the tension, beginning to fuss in her arms. Sarah's mind raced back to high school, to the humiliation that had shaped so much of her adult life.

Victoria had been Sarah's roommate during their senior year at Blue Valley High. She'd tried to push through anyway, but her voice had cracked on the first note, ending any hope of landing the part. Victoria had gotten the role, and had made sure to remind Sarah of it every single day until graduation.

The X-Factor audition had been her way of proving Victoria wrong, of showing the world – and herself – that dreams weren't just for people with connections and trust funds.

"We're going on an adventure, baby girl," Sarah murmured, watching Emma grab at dust motes dancing in the sunlight. "Mama's gonna sing her heart out, and maybe we'll win something beautiful. But no matter what happens, we'll still have each other, okay?"

Emma responded with a toothless grin that seemed to say she was ready for whatever came next.
