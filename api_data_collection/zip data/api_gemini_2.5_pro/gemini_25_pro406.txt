Grief was supposed to have stages. Bargaining, anger, acceptance. Jack’s grief had one stage: obsession. It had started two years ago, the day he’d buried Eleanor. He’d spent a year isolating the resin, rendering it down, mixing it with precise, agonizingly measured drops of raw honey. For what, he hadn't been sure at first. A folk remedy. A madman’s poultice. He opened one of the cages and gently coaxed a small mouse into his palm. It was Number Seven. He knew them all by the tiny nicks he’d put in their ears.
