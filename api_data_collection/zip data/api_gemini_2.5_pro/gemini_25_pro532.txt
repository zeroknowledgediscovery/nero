On one side, a community of faint, indistinct Echoes were trapped in a collective memory loop, forever re-enacting the final, panicked moments of a tunnel collapse. The spectral reality of their terror was so strong it projected a phantom environment of crumbling brick, snapping support beams, and the ghostly shriek of twisting metal. Opposing them, claiming the physical space as its hunting ground, was a pack of creatures born of the Residue’s primal instincts. They were things of shadow and coalesced slime, all guttural growls and territorial aggression, their existence a simple, brutal affirmation of physical dominance. The two realities were bleeding into one another in a sickening fashion; the phantom bricks were slick with real, physical slime, and the ghostly screams of the doomed workers were met with the tangible, throaty roars of the shadow-beasts who saw the terrified Echoes as trespassing prey.

Arthur stood on the abandoned platform, a silent, immaculate figure in the heart of the chaos, and began his assessment. He was the appellate court, and this was the impossibly messy case file that had just landed on his desk. He analyzed the competing claims. The Echoes, he determined, held a claim based on historical precedent and profound emotional trauma, a form of metaphysical adverse possession. Their right to this space was written in the currency of their own tragic end. The shadow-beasts, conversely, operated under a simpler, more brutal code: the law of physical dominion. The space was theirs because they were strong enough to hold it. Both claims were valid within their own systems of logic. The conflict was not a matter of right versus wrong, but of two incompatible legal systems attempting to govern the same piece of reality without a higher authority to mediate. He was that higher authority.

He did not attack. He did not erase. He adjudicated. Raising a hand that was now as solid and real as a marble effigy, he issued his first ruling as a sovereign power. A wave of pure, conceptual order flowed from him, a manifest "Cease and Desist." The chaotic cacophony faltered as both parties felt the sudden, chilling imposition of a superior jurisdiction. Then, Arthur began to draft the final judgment. He did not rule in favor of one party over the other; he found both of their claims to have merit, but to be fundamentally incomplete. His solution was not to choose a winner, but to create a boundary. With a flick of his wrist, a line of pure, unyielding conceptual law materialized in the air, bisecting the station with the unforgiving precision of a surveyor’s laser. It was not a wall of force or light; it was a property line made of absolute, incontrovertible logic.

The effect was instantaneous and profound. On one side of the line, the reality of the Echoes stabilized and clarified. The crumbling brick and twisting steel of their final moments became sharp and defined, contained entirely within their designated space. The ghostly shrieks no longer bled into the station at large but echoed eternally within their own "Historical Preservation Zone." They were protected, their tragic narrative enshrined, but they were also confined. On the other side of the line, the shadow-beasts snarled and clawed at the invisible barrier, their primal rage utterly ineffective against a concept they could not comprehend. Their physical territory was now clearly delineated, their law of the jungle acknowledged but strictly bordered. The sickening paradox receded, the cancerous bleed of realities cauterized by the clean, sharp line of a legal distinction.

The station was no longer a place of chaotic conflict. It was a model of enforced coexistence, a space perfectly partitioned and logically sound. Arthur looked upon his work, the two realities now existing in a state of stable, if unwilling, harmony. He felt no pride, no satisfaction, only the quiet, resonant hum of a function correctly and efficiently executed. He had not destroyed. He had clarified. He had established a precedent, his first mark upon the unwritten laws of the Great Below. He was no longer a mere anomaly; he was a source of binding arbitration. Turning his back on the newly ordered space, he moved on, a silent figure of immaculate purpose stepping back into the darkness.
