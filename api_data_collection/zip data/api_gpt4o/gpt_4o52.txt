**Los Angeles**

The air was unusually crisp that morning as the sun peeked over the horizon, bathing the cityscape of Los Angeles in golden hues. Jaclyn Montgomery, better known to her friends as Jack, pulled on her well-worn leather jacket and hopped down the steps of her apartment building. Her motorcycle gleamed under the early morning light, a trusty companion that had taken her on many a wild adventure. Today, though, was different. Today, she was heading to the airport for a journey that could change her life forever.

Jack was a stud in every sense of the word—confident, unruly dark hair, an effortless swagger in her walk that turned heads wherever she went. But what really set her apart was her voice, a powerhouse instrument that had landed her a coveted spot in the grand finals of Britain's Got Talent. It was an opportunity that seemed almost surreal, an American punk girl having a shot at fame across the pond.

The ride to the airport was uneventful, Los Angeles traffic surprisingly cooperative. Jack couldn't help but daydream about the possibilities ahead. Her mind fluttered with visions of grandeur—performing on stage at the Royal Albert Hall, the thunderous applause of an international audience, and the chance to truly make a name for herself. This was more than a competition; it was a shot at destiny.

Boarding the plane, she felt a rush of excitement mixed with a tinge of unease. There was something about leaving her hometown, the only life she'd known, that left her curiously apprehensive. But Jack was never one to back away from a challenge. Propping her boots against the seat in front of her, she settled in for the long flight.

Upon arriving in London, the chilly air was a welcome change from the heat of California. She was greeted by the bustling energy of the city, a vibrant mix of old architecture and modern innovation. There was no time to lose. The finals were just days away, and every moment counted. She checked into her hotel, a quaint little place that overlooked Hyde Park, and immediately set about rehearsing in the tiny space of her room.

The buzz around the competition was electric. Contestants from all over the world thronged the venue, each a kaleidoscope of talent and ambition. Jack felt a thrill of camaraderie amid the healthy sense of rivalry. This was where she belonged, among the dreamers and the doers, all vying for that one moment of glory.

As the days passed, she fell into a rhythm. Mornings spent rehearsing and evenings exploring the city with a group of fellow competitors who quickly became friends. They'd wander the cobbled streets, her voice soaring as they busked for fun in Covent Garden. Her confidence grew, a fierce intensity burning within her.

Then came the evening of the finals. The theater buzzed with anticipation, lights casting a gentle glow on the assembled hopefuls and expectant audience. Jack’s heart thumped in sync with the energetic hum of the crowd. Text messages from her friends back in Los Angeles poured in, all rooting for her from thousands of miles away.

Dressed in her favorite black ensemble, guitar slung over her shoulder, Jack was ready. As the curtains rose, she felt a wave of calm wash over her. She could do this. She was meant to do this.

Her performance was electric, a blend of edgy rock and soulful blues. The audience was captivated, swept along by her voice as it danced through the air. The judges exchanged glances of surprise, nodding in rhythm, acknowledging the rare talent before them.

The applause was deafening as she took her final bow. The moment, though fleeting, was one she would treasure forever. Standing on that stage, basking in the ovation, she felt invincible.

But fate, unpredictable and capricious, had other plans.

Barely moments after stepping offstage, Jack’s world was abruptly turned upside down. She was suddenly overwhelmed from behind, a hood yanked over her head, plunging her into darkness. Panic surged through her as confusion reigned. Her mind spun, attempting to make sense of what was happening.

She fought, struggled to free herself, but the grips were strong, unyielding. Voices muttered around her, thick with an accent she couldn’t place. The jarring shift from the spotlight to blind captivity was disorienting, leaving her head spinning.

Her captors hustled her into a van, the screech of tires echoing in her ears as they sped off into the unknown. Time dragged on interminably in that blurred state, her thoughts a jumbled mess of fear and frustration.

When the hood was finally removed, she found herself in a dimly lit basement. Her captors, their faces obscured by masks, spoke in low tones, a language barrier preventing any semblance of understanding. Despite the confusion, they didn’t seem intent on harming her, just preventing her from leaving.

As night wore into day, Jack continuously sought means of escape, yet always foiled by vigilant eyes. Her mind kept returning to the competition, to the chances she might miss. Anxiety gnawed at her, coupled with an anger that simmered just below the surface.

Then, in the solitude of her confinement, the situation took an even stranger twist. She hadn’t noticed it at first, consumed as she was by her predicament, but there was something off about one of her captors. A deathly pallor that seemed almost... unnatural.

Her heart skipped a beat, watching in growing horror as the figure lurched toward her with an unsteady gait, something primal and unsettling in its movements. In the dim candlelight, the grotesque truth became clearer—she was staring into the lifeless eyes of a zombie.

Before she could react, it happened fast—a sudden lurch, a searing pain on her arm as teeth sank into her flesh. Jack’s scream filled the room, raw and piercing, a sound born of shock and a fear she could barely comprehend.

Her captors, panicked, restrained the zombie but the damage was done. Her mind grappled with the implications, a cascade of incredulity and dread. None of it made sense, the specter of infection now an inescapable reality.

Hours stretched on. She felt it, a creeping sensation unlike any other, something coursing through her veins, insidious and consuming. Her thoughts grew foggy, memories slipping like sand through her fingers. A feverish need settled deep within her.

By the time of the finals, Jack had been standing on the precipice of her dreams, only to face this unfathomable nightmare. The bite disqualified her, an ironclad rule against infection barring any hope of returning to the stage.

Back in her hotel room, the vibrant city outside was a world away, a haunting reminder of ambition thwarted. The competition continued, oblivious to her ordeal—yet Jack Montgomery, the stud with the incredible voice, was part of it no longer.

In between bouts of dismay and hollow acceptance, a resolve began to form. She wouldn’t let this be her end. There was a strength within her yet, a tenacity born of the very city she hailed from. Los Angeles had taught her resilience, and she clung to it now with all her might.
