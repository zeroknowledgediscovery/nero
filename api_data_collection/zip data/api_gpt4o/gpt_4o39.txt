Growing up in the secluded town of Montcairn, Catherine "Cath" Whittaker always seemed to linger at the fringes of society. Etched in mystery and solitude, her existence was a peculiar dance of shadows and whispers. From her bleak house perched on the hill, she gazed down at the townsfolk, wistfully observing the flow of their mundane lives. But nothing stirred her curiosity quite like the children. Those sprightly, innocent souls, gliding through their days with such lighthearted abandon.

Cath's envy bloomed like a clandestine flower, watered by the tears of her discontent. The town did little to quell her jealousy. Parents watched with wary eyes and whispered warnings to their children, creating legends of the Troll Lady, who lurked unseen but ever-watchful.

Occupying her days with an odd mixture of artistic pursuits, Cath discovered a peculiar talent for painting. In her secluded studio, she created canvas worlds tinged with surrealism—most notably, the haunting series of children painted in bright, unnatural shades of pink. Each stroke of her brush was driven by an obsession with the elusive essence of childhood, a long-lost fragment she never captured in her own youth.

Meanwhile, a few towns away in the bustling locale of Ajax, Detective Inspector Eloise Greenlay was far from content. Devoting her life to philanthropy and justice, she had already left a mark on the world, earning prestige and adulation. Yet, she longed for a challenge that would truly test her mettle. Over the years, Greenlay had encountered snippets of Cath's legend; these tales conjured a curiosity that refused to be sated.

It was on a particularly overcast afternoon when fate intervened, drawing Greenlay's attention toward the obscure happenings in Montcairn. Reports of children returning home with bizarre, colorful markings on their skin reached her office, piquing her interest. Some dismissed these as mere rumors—a child's fanciful embellishments—but Greenlay suspected there was more to the tale.

Setting aside skepticism, Greenlay embarked on her journey to Montcairn, eager to uncover the truth behind the peculiar tales shadowing Cath's name. Upon arrival, she quickly delved into the heart of local life, speaking to families and piecing together fragments of the puzzle. Her investigation led her to encounter Thomas Marlborough, the loquacious innkeeper, whose eyes twinkled with the mischievous knowledge of local lore.

"Ah, the Troll Lady, you say?" Thomas's voice brimmed with mirth as he poured Greenlay a cup of steaming tea. "You'll find no more intriguing story in all of Montcairn."

"I'm more interested in the truth behind the story," Greenlay replied, the edges of her patience grating against the innkeeper's leisurely tone.

Thomas leaned closer, the scent of tobacco lingering in the folds of his worn coat. "Children, you see—they go wandering near the woods, and sometimes they come home with tales of being watched. Some claim to see her, lurking. Others, well, they say she marks them with that ungodly pink paint."

"Have you seen her yourself?" Greenlay inquired, intrigued by the possibility.

"Me? No, but the way the village ladies prattle on, you'd think a ghost was looming over these hills every night."

Dark legends or not, there was enough of a pattern to suggest Cath's hand in these bizarre tales. Practicality warred with imagination as Greenlay resolved that the child within their grasp deserved safety from the shadows that danced just out of reach.

Dawn arrived on wings of mist, wrapping Montcairn in a cocoon of gray. Greenlay rose with determination nestled in her chest. Trudging her way up the steep path toward Cath's home, she couldn't shake the feeling that an unseen presence was meticulously charting her progress.

Cath, meanwhile, stood at the window of her studio, her weathered fingers stained with watercolors. The unwelcome intrusion of reality was a disruption she neither asked for nor desired.
